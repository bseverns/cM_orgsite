About createMPLS

STEM access is still uneven. createMPLS exists to close that distance.

The organization is built around a direct premise: students should not have to wait for a perfectly resourced school to experience quality STEM learning. createMPLS brings that experience closer and lowers the barriers that keep it out of reach.

Local and direct The work is framed around real schools, community sites, and operating relationships.
Barrier-aware Cost, transportation, equipment, and exposure gaps are treated as real parts of the access challenge.
The problem

The tech-education gap is structural, not accidental.

Many students in low-income communities, communities of color, and schools carrying persistent resource constraints simply do not receive the same exposure to STEM tools, teachers, and pathways that other students take for granted.

That gap shows up early. By the time students are expected to imagine themselves in technical fields, many have already had fewer chances to experiment, build, and see what technical work actually looks like.

What uneven access can look like
  • Schools that cannot consistently fund equipment or specialized STEM instruction
  • Students who need transportation or extra family coordination to reach enrichment
  • Young women and students of color seeing fewer clear signals that they belong in technical spaces
  • Communities carrying demand for hands-on learning without stable delivery capacity
Operating model

How createMPLS responds

The model is practical, partner-driven, and designed for settings where students already show up.

1. Partner

Start with schools and community organizations

Programs begin with a site that already knows its students, schedule, and local constraints.

2. Deliver

Bring instructors, tools, and structure on site

Delivery happens where students already are, reducing the extra logistics that often cut participation first.

3. Engage

Use hands-on learning as the core experience

Students build, test, code, iterate, and collaborate instead of being limited to abstract exposure.

Values in practice

The work is shaped by a few non-negotiable principles.

These values explain how createMPLS approaches partnership, student belonging, and the practical question of who gets access.

Access

No-cost participation

Access is strongest when cost is designed out, not patched over later.

Belonging

Students should see themselves in the work

The language stays clear that young women and students of color belong in technical learning environments.

Partnership

Schools are collaborators, not backdrops

createMPLS is framed as a complement to schools and community sites, not a replacement for them.
