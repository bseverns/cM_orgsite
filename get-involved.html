Get involved

Support the work in a way that fits what you can offer.

Some people bring a school or community site. Some bring time. Some bring funding or sponsorship. Every path matters when the goal is wider, more consistent STEM access.

Three core paths
  • Start or expand a school or community partnership
  • Volunteer your time, expertise, or classroom support
  • Donate or sponsor access so the no-cost model can hold
Choose a pathway

Each support path should be easy to recognize.

Whether you want to host programming, volunteer your time, or fund access, the next step should feel specific and manageable.

Partnership details

Good fit for schools, youth organizations, and community hosts

Partnership conversations focus on who your site serves, what age range you need to support, and whether school-day or enrichment programming makes the most sense.

Volunteer details

Good fit for supporters who want a direct student-facing role

Volunteers help widen what students can experience, whether that means encouraging problem-solving, helping with projects, or sharing technical perspective.

Funding details

Good fit for individual donors, teams, and sponsors

Funding supports the operating conditions that keep programs reachable: equipment, instruction, and the infrastructure of no-cost delivery.

What happens next

Lower-friction follow-through matters more than broad language.

A clear next step matters. People are more likely to follow through when they know what happens after they click.

1

Choose the closest-fit inquiry path

The contact form can be preselected for partnership, volunteer, sponsor, donor, or general inquiries.

2

Share a little context

People can describe their school, role, organization, or the kind of support they want to offer.

3

Continue the conversation directly

Supporters can reach out through the form or move into a direct conversation without being pushed through a complicated process.

"The strongest support message is not 'help us grow.' It is 'help students reach work they should already have access to explore.'"
createMPLS support principle
Why support matters

Support keeps access practical, not theoretical.

Support matters because it protects the conditions students feel most directly: enough partner capacity, enough student-facing time, and enough funding to keep the model free to families.
