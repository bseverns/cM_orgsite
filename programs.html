Programs

Programs designed for real school and community settings.

createMPLS offers two core program tracks that help partner sites choose the right mix of structured school-day delivery and flexible enrichment. Both are designed to keep access barriers low and student participation practical.

Two tracks School-day curriculum and learning labs work together instead of competing for space.
Partner-ready Each path is built so schools and community sites can see where it fits.
Program overview

Two ways to build sustained STEM access.

Each offer is built around real delivery conditions: where it fits, how it works, and what it makes possible for students and partner sites.

In-school STEM curriculum

For schools that need consistent, school-day delivery

createMPLS brings hands-on technology learning into the school day so students can participate without needing an extra transportation or after-hours entry point.

  • Useful when a school wants structured STEM instruction on site
  • Built around hands-on engineering, robotics, coding, and iteration
  • Supports students across the K-12 span with age-appropriate progression
STEM learning labs

For before school, after school, summer, and community enrichment

Learning labs open up more flexible exploration and give students room to try tools, build curiosity, and go deeper through enrichment settings.

  • Designed for flexible scheduling and mixed readiness levels
  • Good fit for schools, nonprofits, and community organizations
  • Supports discovery across robotics, coding, fabrication, and project-based work
What partners can expect

Schools and organizations should know what the model asks of them.

Schools and community organizations usually need more than inspiration. They need a clear sense of how the relationship actually works.

Planning

Program fit starts with your setting

Conversations begin with student age range, site schedule, staff context, and what level of delivery support is most helpful.

Delivery

Instruction is designed to show up on site

createMPLS brings structure, instruction, and support where students already are.

Tools

Hands-on equipment is part of the learning model

Students work with actual tools and projects rather than receiving STEM as a purely abstract idea.

Tools and experiences

From entry points to deeper exploration.

Students may encounter tools and platforms including LEGO Education, coding environments, robotics systems, 3D printing, and other hands-on STEM equipment depending on the program setting.

LEGO Education Robotics Coding 3D printing Design thinking Collaborative problem solving
  • Students work through real builds, debugging, iteration, and presentation
  • Programs are meant to support both skill development and confidence
  • Hands-on learning is treated as the core method, not a visual extra
"The strongest program story is not that students were exposed to STEM. It is that they had repeated chances to make, test, revise, and keep going."
Program learning principle
Access design

No-cost participation is not a footnote.

The no-cost model, on-site delivery, and school-community fit are part of what makes the programs credible for the students they are meant to reach.

Cost

Free to students and families

Public messaging keeps cost visible because it directly affects who gets access.

Logistics

Delivery aligned with where students already are

On-site programming can reduce friction that otherwise limits participation first.

Audience

Designed for students historically left out of STEM access

The programs are built for students who have historically had less access to consistent, hands-on STEM learning.
