Why createMPLS exists

Talent is not the scarce resource. Access is.

Students in under-resourced schools are often expected to compete in a technology-shaped world without consistent access to modern tools, dedicated STEM instruction, or the space to imagine themselves in technical fields.

createMPLS closes that distance by bringing instruction, equipment, and hands-on learning directly into schools and community sites. The model is practical: meet students where they already are, reduce the barriers that keep them out, and make STEM feel reachable early.

How partnership works

A school or community partnership should feel clear from the first conversation.

If your school or organization is trying to widen access, the next step should be clear: start a conversation about your students, your setting, and the kind of support that will actually work.

Step 1

Start with your site and student needs

Use the contact flow to describe your school, community organization, learner age range, and scheduling reality.

Step 2

Shape the right program model

createMPLS can fit into the school day, before or after school, summer programming, or community-based enrichment.

Step 3

Deliver hands-on learning with fewer barriers

Instruction, tools, and hands-on project work show up on site so students can participate without extra transportation or family cost.

Programs

Two program tracks, one shared purpose.

Both offerings are built to help students practice technical skills, teamwork, iteration, and confidence with real tools.

In-school STEM curriculum

School-day learning that fits existing schedules

Partner schools can bring createMPLS into the school day for hands-on engineering and technology learning without asking students to find a separate entry point.

  • Progressive learning from early builders to older robotics and coding work
  • Structured around design, iteration, and collaboration
  • Useful when schools want reliable, direct delivery support
STEM learning labs

Flexible enrichment for before school, after school, and summer

Labs create room for broader exploration across coding, robotics, fabrication, and other STEM experiences that help students discover what interests them.

  • Built for enrichment settings and mixed readiness levels
  • Includes room for curiosity, experimentation, and team experiences
  • Useful for community sites as well as school partners
